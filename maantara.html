Brand strategy · Wellness

pleasure. power. peace.

Maantara

Sexual wellness has never really had space for survivors. Everyone deserves a place to belong — so I built one.

The problem

Sexual wellness as an industry is either clinical and cold, or pleasure-forward and unprepared for anyone carrying trauma. Neither serves the people who need it most.

81%
of women have experienced sexual harassment or assault in their lifetime
57%
say it started before they turned 17
73%
of survivors report challenges with intimacy afterward
12%
of wellness platforms take a trauma-informed approach

What people actually told me

I ran interviews and a survey to understand where the gap really was. What came back wasn't a request for more information — it was a request for care.

"I'm curious, but it's overwhelming. I don't see myself in any of this, and I still don't know where to start. I've added things to my cart so many times, but I never check out. I just don't know what to trust."
"It's not that I don't want to feel better — I just need tools that actually understand what I've been through. Not clinical. Not performative. Just human."

The brand

Maantara exists to reclaim sexual wellness as a path to healing, dignity, and self-worth — for every body, not just the ones the industry was built around.

Pleasure

Reclaiming joy, desire, and bodily autonomy without shame.

Power

Inner strength, choice, and self-definition.

Peace

Emotional safety, nervous system regulation, and long-term healing.

The opportunity

The global sexual wellness market is expected to reach $125.1 billion by 2026, growing at a 12.4% CAGR.

Source: businesswire.com, cited in original research

Nobody affordable and mainstream is building specifically for survivors. That's not a niche — it's most of the market, underserved.

Let's work together

Let's build work that answers to people.

The world we owe each other isn't going to design itself.
