More work

Five more projects, same question underneath all of them: whose needs is this actually serving?

Brand strategy

e.l.f.

e.l.f. said it was for everyone. I designed what that actually looks like — across ages, emotions, and the moments between. A brand strategy project reimagining e.l.f. Beauty's next chapter through emotional resonance and cultural relevance instead of just affordability and virality.

Product concept

Nebula

Shoot for the stars, land on love. A dating app concept built around character-first, verified connection instead of appearance-first swiping — two-step verification, buddy dating, and a five-meet streak before things turn romantic.

I led strategy and research. Peilan Liang led visual design.

Community mobilization

CRSA

Founded during the second wave of COVID-19 in India with two other students. Over three months the team grew to 400 volunteers and helped roughly 300 people find verified oxygen, medicine, and hospital beds. The work was later recognized in Elle India.

Product design

Sakhi

Banana fiber-based sanitary pads designed for cost and access in urban Ahmedabad, where only 121 million of 366 million menstruating women have access to sanitary napkins. Grounded in ethnographic interviews with the women the product was actually for.

Service design

CybersafeIndia

A platform to help students navigate cyberbullying, built after learning that only half of adolescents who experience it ever report it. It synthesizes legal help, mental health support, and reporting into one accessible path — because the resources already existed, they were just scattered across places no scared teenager was going to find on their own.
